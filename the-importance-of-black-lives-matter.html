Expel Stereotypes: Race/Discrimination

The Importance of Black Lives Matter

Adeen Ali
June 29, 2020

Questions to be raised. Answers to be given. Why does no one stand in the shoe of those who have been hated by the world without any reason? Is it too hard or is too weird? This is absolutely what is happening in the world right now! All the racism victims have been hated, cursed and tortured to death without any reason!

Why did there a time in this world come , when people had to protest about Black Lives? Are they not humans? Do they have no rights to live? Or do their lives not matter? This is where humanity dies, this is where justice ends and this is where equality finishes! Did we all forget about George Floyd, who was killed, because of racism. After hearing this, I was fully out-raged and another question raised in my mind that what is the difference between white skin or black skin when they both have the same colored blood?

"Why are people colour-coded?"

Yes, this is what really matters. I wish that one day, all the people in this world understand the meaning of Black Lives Matter. I wait for that day to come when all the black children are allowed to play with the white children. I hope that one day, everyone will understand the importance of black lives.

Let us not ignore Barack Obama, the first American President. Hank Aaron, an American retired MLB right fielder. Ira Aldridge, an American and British actor who made his career on London stage. Willow Smith who was a songwriter, record producer and an actress from US. They all and many more people like them had reasons to live and they proved that color shade of humans doesn't matter.

Martin Luther Kung once said, “I have a dream that my four children one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by their color, but the content of their character.” So let us fulfil his dream and create a world where a book is never judged by its cover but it’s content. In the end I repeat “BLACK LIVES REALLY MATTER!”
