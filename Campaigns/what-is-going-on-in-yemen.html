What is Going On in Yemen?

Yemen is facing the worst humanitarian crisis in the world.

The meaning of "humanitarian disaster is: "is an event or series of events that represents a critical threat to the health, safety, security or wellbeing of a community or other large group of people, usually over a wide area."
Yemen is currently facing three disastrous events all at once (keep in mind, they were one of the poorest countries to begin with!)

Their own epidemic (country-wide) spreads of cholera, malaria, dengue fever and diphtheria.

Dealing with the (worldwide) pandemic of Covid-19 while having little capacity to test those suspected of having the virus and a destroyed health system.

An ongoing 5 year civil war that has decimated the whole country and left millions displaced and in dire need of assistance.

YEMEN FACTS AND FIGURES

  • 24 million in need of humanitarian assistance
  • 20.1 million people who are food insecure
  • 19.7 million in need of basic healthcare
  • 17.8 million in need of water and sanitation
  • 3 million acutely malnourished children, pregnant or lactating women
  • 3.3 million internally displaced people
